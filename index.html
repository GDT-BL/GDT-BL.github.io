Goal-driven transformer for behavior learning

Abstract

Robot behavior learning has emerged as a crucial field, allowing robots to adapt and improve their actions based on experiential knowledge rather than being solely reliant on predefined instructions. While machine learning algorithms have been instrumental in computer vision and language domain, adapting them for robot play data remains a significant challenge. Although reinforcement learning based approaches have obtained satisfying performance, they neces- sitate task-specific datasets with reward labels. To overcome these challenges, we propose a Goal-Driven Transformer (GDT) architecture for robot behavior learning from multimodal play data. The GDT model comprises three essential components: the embedding layer, goal-driven attention block, and standard attention block. Among them, the goal-driven attention module is the core module of GDT, which explicitly integrates the desired goal into the attention mechanism, enabling the model to selectively focus on critical parts of the observation data to perform behavioral learning for specific goals. As a result, the robot can tailor its behavior to meet high-level objectives or instructions, offering enhanced adaptability and versatility. Experimental validation of the proposed GDT framework is conducted in two simulated environments: Multi-modal Block- Pushing and Franka Relay Kitchen. The results demonstrate that the GDT framework has achieved state-of-the-art perfor- mance in the realm of robot behavior learning from play data.
